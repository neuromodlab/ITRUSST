Summary

After discussing the strategy for upcoming reports, we established that our consensus protocol is as it currently already works with two major changes to what we previously suggested. The first change is I) we won't ask for a vote of all of the members and we won't have any separation of who's a member or active or not and the second change is II) that we will distribute the consensus proposal within ITRUSST but also widely and actively look for detailed feedback - even on that proposal that the working group will continue to pick up and work on.

Let's remind everybody of the consensus protocol. This is what we agreed upon about a year ago and that we prepared the year before. We say consensus is needed for these recommendations or guidelines and it goes in two stages. We work on the proposal of working groups and then at the plenary meetings, we vote, and we would like to keep that separate. The definition of consensus here is that 50 of the voting members should put in their vote and a consensus is reached if more than 70 percent of them are yay voters. We had less than 10 veto against (if somebody objects from the working group). The voting was done by anonymous electronic votes. One critical aspect is that we decided back then that only active members had voting right. An active member is somebody who is a members of one the working groups, the rational is we wanted ITRUSST to be open and inclusive for everybody to be an observer, even if you’re in starting but if you’re an active contributor to some of the working groups, you have a stake in gaming as you also have expertise.
